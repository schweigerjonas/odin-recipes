Tomahawk Steak

Description

Enjoy smoky flavors with this succulent Smoke-Grilled Tomahawk, served with charred broccoli for a hearty and satisfying meal.

Ingredients

Steak

  • Tomahawk Steak - 1 piece
  • Grapeseed Oil - as needed
  • Salt & Pepper - to taste

Broccoli

  • Broccoli Florets with Stem - 4 cups
  • Olive Oil - 3tbsp
  • Grapeseed Oil - 2tbsp
  • Chopped Garlic - 1tbsp
  • Chopped Oregano - 1tbsp
  • Chopped Thyme - 2tsp
  • Cider Vinegar - 1/3 cup
  • Sugar - 2tbsp
  • Wholegrain Mustard - 1tsp
  • Salt & Pepper - to taste

Sweet Potato

  • Par-baked Sweet Potato - 4 medium pieces
  • Butter - 4tbsp
  • Crème Fraiche - 1/2 cup
  • Honey - 2tbsp
  • Toasted Pine Nuts - 4tbsp
  • Salt & Pepper - to taste

Steps

Broccoli

  1. In a small pot, gently heat the olive oil and grapeseed oil.
  2. Add garlic and cook until lightly golden.
  3. Add the herbs and allow to sizzle for a few seconds.
  4. Add vinegar, sugar, mustard, and season with salt and pepper. Cook for a few minutes on low heat until the sugar dissolves. Toss the grilled broccoli with the warm vinaigrette.

Sweet Potato

  1. Wrap each par-baked sweet potato in foil with 1 tbsp butter in each parcel.
  2. Put on the grill in a spot without direct heat. Allow to finish cooking until tender.
  3. To serve, split the sweet potato, add crème fraiche, pine nuts, drizzle with honey and sprinkle with sea salt and black pepper.

Steak

  1. Allow tomahawk to come to room temperature, covered, for about 30 minutes.
  2. Pat steak completely dry, several times as needed.
  3. Very lightly rub grapeseed oil on steak, not enough to drip off, just enough for the seasoning to stick.
  4. Season liberally with salt and pepper all over.
  5. Place on a pre-heated hot grill. Depending on what kind of grill you have, cooking directions will vary. I have a pellet smoker grill, so I start with a low smoke for 30-40 minutes and reverse sear on a hot grill.
  6. After searing, remove steak from grill and rest (under a tent of foil, if you like) when you are 5-10 degrees from the desired temperature. I like medium-rare so I will cook it on the grill until the internal temperature is 49-50C / 120-122F, allowing for residual heat to bring it up to 53-54C / 128-130F.
